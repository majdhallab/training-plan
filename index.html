Coaching · Rules · Protocol
Morning Routine
Every Day Mon–Sat
50 min fasted walk before eating anything. Fasted cardio pulls from fat stores — liver glycogen is depleted overnight. HR 115–130. Immediately after: 10 min abs circuit (crunches + leg raises + plank). Then breakfast. This is your daily non-negotiable. Rain, work, stress — it still happens. Sunday is the only exception.
Post-Session Walk
Mon · Tue · Wed
30 min brisk walk immediately after your session. Glycogen is depleted from lifting — the body pulls from fat at higher rate now. HR 115–130. Do not skip. This is a second fat-burning window on every training day.
Fri · Sat
Treadmill / Stairmaster at end of session is your post-session walk. You're already doing it. No extra walk needed on these days.
The Split — Why It Works
Legs Twice Weekly
Legs = 60% of your muscle mass. Every leg session creates massive EPOC (metabolism elevated 24–48 hrs after). Monday heavy, Friday moderate with 4-day gap = full recovery + maximum fat loss. This is the single highest-impact decision in the program.
Thursday Rest is Strategic
Not random. Specifically placed to give full leg recovery before Friday's second leg session. Muscle is built during rest, not training. Protecting Thursday makes Friday better.
Arms Frequency
Triceps hit as secondary on Push. Biceps hit as secondary on Pull. Saturday bonus = one dedicated arms day when muscles are completely fresh. One true arms day is enough for fat loss goals. Arms are small muscles — they don't drive fat loss. Legs do.
Form Rules
Pull Day — Back
Drive elbows, not hands. Retract scapula before pulling. Use straps on all rows. Full stretch at top of lat pulldown. Biceps are a finisher — don't try to curl your way through back day.
Legs
Leg press 15–20 reps is deliberate — higher reps = more metabolic stress, more lactic acid, more calories burned. This is fat loss training, not powerlifting. Friday is 10–15% lighter than Monday — same movements, reduced load, shorter rest.
Incline DB Curl
3 second negative (slow down). This is where the growth stimulus happens. Don't rush the lowering phase.
Face Pulls
Never skip. Balances all pressing from Push day. Rear delts + rotator cuff health. These keep your shoulders pain-free long term.
Skull Crushers
Stop immediately if you feel elbow pain. Switch to cable overhead tricep extension instead.
Progressive Overload
The Rule
10 reps with 20kg last session → try 10 reps with 22.5kg this session. Log your weights every session. Small increments compound fast. If you can't add weight, add a rep. If you can't add a rep, hold the weight longer at peak contraction.
Phasing
Weeks 1–4 · Foundation
3 sets per exercise. Rest 90–120 sec. Focus on form and building the walk habit. Goal: 50 min AM walk every single day by end of week 2.
Weeks 5–8 · Load
3–4 sets. Rest 60–90 sec. Add weight every session where possible. Body has adapted — now load it.
Weeks 9–12 · Intensify
4 sets. Rest 45–60 sec. Supersets on all sessions. Shorter rest = more metabolic stress = more fat burn. Phase 3 is where the body changes visibly.
Daily Non-Negotiables
Numbers to Hit Every Day
3–4L water · 2g protein per kg bodyweight · 7–8 hrs sleep · 300–500 kcal deficit from food

The walks + training create ~4,000 kcal burn per week from exercise. Add a modest food deficit on top. The combination is unstoppable.
Priority Order — If You Miss a Day
Never skip Monday Legs. It's the highest calorie burn session of the week and sets the metabolic tone. If you can only train 3 days: Mon + Tue + Wed. If you can only train 2: Mon + Fri. Legs always first.
